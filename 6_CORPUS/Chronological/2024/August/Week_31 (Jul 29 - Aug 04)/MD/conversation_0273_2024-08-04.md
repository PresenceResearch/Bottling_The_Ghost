Here are some great audiobooks you might enjoy before your trip to Japan:

### Historical Fiction:
1. **"Shogun" by James Clavell**  
   A sweeping epic that brings to life the feudal era of Japan in the 1600s through the eyes of a shipwrecked Englishman.

2. **"The Samurai’s Garden" by Gail Tsukiyama**  
   This novel beautifully intertwines the themes of love, war, and personal growth set against the backdrop of Japan in the 1930s.

3. **"Memoirs of a Geisha" by Arthur Golden**  
   A rich historical novel that delves into the life of a geisha in Kyoto during the pre- and post-World War II period.

### Japanese History:
4. **"The Rape of Nanking" by Iris Chang**  
   A detailed and powerful account of the atrocities committed during the Japanese invasion of Nanking in 1937.

5. **"Embracing Defeat: Japan in the Wake of World War II" by John W. Dower**  
   This book provides an in-depth look at Japan’s transformation after its defeat in World War II.

6. **"Hirohito and the Making of Modern Japan" by Herbert P. Bix**  
   A comprehensive biography that examines the life of Emperor Hirohito and Japan’s history throughout the 20th century.

### Japanese Culture:
7. **"The Book of Tea" by Kakuzo Okakura**  
   A classic work exploring the history, philosophy, and cultural significance of tea in Japan.

8. **"Autumn Light: Season of Fire and Farewells" by Pico Iyer**  
   A poignant reflection on family, aging, and the subtle beauty of everyday life in Japan.

### Modern Japan:
9. **"Tokyo Vice: An American Reporter on the Police Beat in Japan" by Jake Adelstein**  
   A thrilling account of crime and journalism in Japan from an American reporter who worked for a Japanese newspaper.

10. **"A Tale for the Time Being" by Ruth Ozeki**  
    A contemporary novel that weaves together the stories of a Japanese teenager and a writer in British Columbia.

### World War II Perspective:
11. **"Japan at War: An Oral History" by Haruko Taya Cook and Theodore F. Cook**  
    An insightful collection of personal accounts from Japanese soldiers, civilians, and politicians who lived through World War II.

12. **"The Pacific War 1931-1945" by Saburo Ienaga**  
    A critical examination of Japan’s involvement in World War II from the Japanese perspective.

These audiobooks should give you a well-rounded understanding of Japan's history, culture, and society as you prepare for your trip. Let me know if there's a specific topic you'd like to explore more!
