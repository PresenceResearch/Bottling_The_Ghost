The Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SREC) program is a market-based instrument that provides financial incentives for the generation of renewable energy, particularly solar power. Here’s a detailed explanation of how the SREC program works:

### How SRECs are Created
1. **Generation**: When a solar energy system generates electricity, it produces SRECs based on the amount of electricity generated. Typically, one SREC is equivalent to one megawatt-hour (MWh) of electricity (1,000 kilowatt-hours (kWh)).

### SREC Markets and Sales
2. **Market Value**: The value of an SREC is determined by market demand and supply, which can fluctuate. Prices can be influenced by state regulations, the overall demand for renewable energy, and the number of SRECs available in the market.
3. **Trading**: SRECs can be sold or traded in an open market. System owners can sell their SRECs directly to utilities, brokers, or through SREC marketplaces. In some states, utilities are required to purchase a certain number of SRECs to comply with state renewable portfolio standards (RPS).

### SREC Procurement Programs
4. **Procurement Programs**: Some states or utilities run SREC procurement programs, where they purchase SRECs through competitive bidding processes. For example, in Delaware, Delmarva Power operates an annual SREC procurement program where they solicit bids from SREC sellers and award long-term contracts to the lowest bidders.

### Compliance and Reporting
5. **Compliance**: Utilities and energy suppliers use SRECs to meet the requirements set by the state’s RPS. RPS mandates that a certain percentage of the electricity sold by these entities must come from renewable sources. By purchasing SRECs, utilities can demonstrate compliance with these mandates.
6. **Reporting**: Solar system owners or aggregators report the amount of electricity generated to a tracking system that issues SRECs based on verified production data.

### Benefits for Solar System Owners
7. **Revenue Stream**: Selling SRECs provides an additional revenue stream for solar system owners, helping to offset the initial cost of installing solar panels. This can significantly improve the return on investment for solar projects.
8. **Encouraging Solar Adoption**: The financial incentive provided by SRECs encourages more individuals and businesses to invest in solar energy, contributing to the growth of renewable energy capacity.

### Delaware Specifics
- **Annual Procurement**: Delmarva Power, in collaboration with the Delaware Sustainable Energy Utility (DESEU), conducts an annual procurement program to purchase SRECs. Solar system owners submit bids, and the lowest bids are accepted until the procurement goals are met.
- **SREC Bonuses**: In Delaware, additional bonuses can be awarded if the solar system uses Delaware-manufactured parts or is installed using Delaware labor.

Overall, the SREC program is a key mechanism in promoting the adoption of solar energy by providing financial incentives to solar system owners, thereby supporting state renewable energy goals and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
