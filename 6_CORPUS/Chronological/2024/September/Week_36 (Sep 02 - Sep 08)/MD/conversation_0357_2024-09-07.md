#### **Course Description:**

This course offers a comprehensive survey of American musical theater, tracing its development from the 19th century to the present day. The course will explore key composers, lyricists, librettists, choreographers, directors, and performers, as well as landmark productions and the socio-cultural contexts in which they were created. Through a mix of lectures, listening/viewing assignments, and class discussions, students will gain an understanding of the musical, lyrical, and theatrical elements that define the genre.

#### **Course Objectives:**

By the end of the course, students will be able to:  
1. Identify and discuss the key historical and cultural milestones in the development of American musical theater.
2. Analyze the structure, style, and themes of representative works.
3. Understand the contributions of significant composers, lyricists, and other creative personnel to the development of the genre.
4. Critically evaluate the social, political, and cultural influences on musical theater.
5. Develop an appreciation for the diversity and evolution of the American musical theater genre.

#### **Weekly Schedule:**

**Week 1: Introduction to American Musical Theater**  
- Course Overview, Expectations, and Introduction  
- Early influences: Operetta, Minstrel Shows, and Vaudeville  
- Viewing: Selected clips from early musicals  
- Reading: Introduction and Chapter 1 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 2: The Birth of the American Musical: 1900-1930**  
- Early Musical Comedy and Revue (e.g., "The Ziegfeld Follies," "Shuffle Along")  
- The Rise of Tin Pan Alley and the Jazz Age  
- Key Figures: George M. Cohan, Irving Berlin, Eubie Blake  
- Reading: Chapter 2 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 3: The Golden Age Begins: 1930-1940**  
- The Great Depression and Its Impact on Broadway  
- Innovations of Show Boat and the Integration of Story and Song  
- Key Figures: Jerome Kern, Richard Rodgers, Oscar Hammerstein II  
- Listening: Excerpts from "Show Boat" and "Of Thee I Sing"  

**Week 4: The Rodgers and Hammerstein Era: 1940-1950**  
- The "Integrated Musical": Oklahoma!, Carousel, South Pacific  
- The Musical Play: Complex Characters, Narrative Depth, Social Issues  
- Viewing: Clips from "Oklahoma!" and "South Pacific"  
- Reading: Chapter 3 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 5: The Evolution of Dance in Musical Theater**  
- The Influence of Agnes de Mille and Jerome Robbins  
- Integration of Dance as a Narrative Element (e.g., "West Side Story")  
- Viewing: Excerpts from "Oklahoma!" and "West Side Story"  

**Week 6: The Rise of the Director-Choreographer: 1950-1960**  
- The Emergence of the Director-Choreographer (Jerome Robbins, Bob Fosse)  
- Innovative Shows: "The King and I," "My Fair Lady," "Gypsy"  
- Listening: Excerpts from "Gypsy" and "My Fair Lady"  

**Week 7: MIDTERM EXAM**  
- Midterm review and exam  

**Week 8: The 1960s and the Concept Musical**  
- Key Figures: Stephen Sondheim, Bob Fosse, Hal Prince  
- Landmark Shows: "Cabaret," "Company," "Follies"  
- Reading: Chapter 4 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 9: Rock and the Mega-Musical: 1970-1980**  
- Rock Influence: "Hair," "Jesus Christ Superstar," "Grease"  
- The British Invasion: Andrew Lloyd Webber and Tim Rice  
- Viewing: Clips from "Evita" and "Cats"  

**Week 10: The 1980s and 1990s: Revivals and Mega-Musicals**  
- The Rise of the Mega-Musical ("Les Misérables," "Phantom of the Opera")  
- Disney's Influence on Broadway  
- Reading: Chapters 5 and 6 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 11: The New Millennium: Diversity and Innovation**  
- The Influence of Hip-Hop and New Forms ("Rent," "In the Heights," "Hamilton")  
- New Voices and Diversity in Musical Theater  
- Viewing: Clips from "Hamilton" and "The Color Purple"  

**Week 12: Musical Theater in the Digital Age**  
- The Impact of Technology, Streaming, and Social Media on Broadway  
- The Evolution of Ticketing, Marketing, and Audience Engagement  
- Reading: Chapter 7 of "Broadway: The American Musical"  

**Week 13: The Role of Women and Minorities in Musical Theater**  
- Contributions of Female and Minority Creatives  
- Analyzing Works by Lin-Manuel Miranda, Jeanine Tesori, and More  
- Listening/Viewing: Excerpts from "Fun Home" and "Hadestown"  

**Week 14: Contemporary Trends and Future Directions**  
- Current Broadway Trends: Jukebox Musicals, Revivals, Experimental Works  
- Predictions for the Future of the Genre  
- Guest Speaker (if available): A Contemporary Musical Theater Professional  

**Week 15: Student Presentations and Final Exam Review**  
- Student Presentations on Research Projects  
- Final Exam Review  

**Week 16: FINAL EXAM**  
- Comprehensive exam covering all course material
