For your Director of Commercial Sales to effectively present this opportunity to the CRO and COO, they should follow a structured approach to ensure the concept is well-communicated and aligns with company goals. Here's how they can do it:

---

### **Preparation**
1. **Understand the Opportunity**:
   - Collect all relevant details about the partnership, including the scope of work, potential revenue, timelines, and any known risks.
   - Assess how the opportunity aligns with the company’s strategic goals, especially in rebuilding the commercial pipeline or addressing gaps in current operations.

2. **Research the Partner**:
   - Gather background information about the potential partner, including their reputation, capabilities, financial stability, and alignment with Lumina Solar’s mission and values.

3. **Evaluate the Impact**:
   - Quantify the potential benefits (e.g., revenue, MW installed, operational efficiencies).
   - Highlight any competitive advantages the partnership might provide.

4. **Prepare Supporting Materials**:
   - Create a concise deck or document outlining the opportunity, including key metrics, projected outcomes, and a proposed action plan.

---

### **Presentation Format**
1. **Set the Context**:
   - Start by framing the opportunity within the company's current commercial strategy.
   - Briefly explain why this is a timely and relevant prospect.

2. **Define the Partnership**:
   - Clearly outline the details of the partnership, including:
     - **Who**: The partner and their role.
     - **What**: The scope of work or project details.
     - **When**: Expected timelines.
     - **Where**: Geographical or market focus.
     - **How**: Proposed collaboration model (e.g., revenue-sharing, subcontracting, etc.).

3. **Highlight the Benefits**:
   - Share specific benefits such as increased revenue, new market entry, operational synergies, or reduced costs.
   - Include projections and key performance indicators (KPIs) that show the potential impact on the company.

4. **Discuss Challenges and Risks**:
   - Address potential risks (e.g., resource allocation, contractual terms, execution hurdles).
   - Propose mitigation strategies to build confidence in the idea’s feasibility.

5. **Propose Next Steps**:
   - Suggest clear, actionable next steps for evaluation and implementation.
   - Include milestones, deliverables, and any additional information needed to proceed.

---

### **Presentation Tips**
- **Keep It Concise**: Focus on high-impact points and leave time for questions.
- **Tailor the Message**: Address the concerns and interests of both the CRO (growth, sales alignment) and the COO (operational feasibility, resource allocation).
- **Engage in Discussion**: Encourage feedback and be prepared to answer detailed questions.
- **Stay Solution-Oriented**: Present the opportunity as a pathway to solving a specific problem or advancing a strategic goal.

---

### **Follow-Up**
1. Summarize key takeaways and agreed next steps in a follow-up email.
2. Share any additional materials or data requested during the discussion.
3. Schedule periodic updates to track progress if the opportunity is approved for further exploration.

---

This structured approach ensures that the presentation is professional, thorough, and actionable, increasing the likelihood of gaining support for the partnership.
