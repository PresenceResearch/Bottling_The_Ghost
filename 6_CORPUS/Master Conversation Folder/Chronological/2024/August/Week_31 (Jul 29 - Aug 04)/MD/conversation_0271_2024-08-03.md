Here are some recommended books about Japan, covering various aspects of its history, culture, and society, as well as historical fiction that might interest you:

### Historical Fiction

1. **"Memoirs of a Geisha" by Arthur Golden**  
   - This novel tells the fictional story of a geisha working in Kyoto before and after World War II, offering a vivid depiction of Japanese culture and history.

2. **"The Samurai" by Shūsaku Endō**  
   - A historical novel that follows a low-ranking samurai on a diplomatic mission to Mexico and Europe in the early 17th century, exploring themes of culture clash and faith.

3. **"Cloud of Sparrows" by Takashi Matsuoka**  
   - Set in the late 19th century, this novel weaves a story of intrigue and adventure, blending samurai traditions with the arrival of Western influence in Japan.

4. **"Silence" by Shūsaku Endō**  
   - This powerful novel is set in the 17th century and follows Portuguese Jesuit missionaries who face persecution in Japan, exploring themes of faith and cultural conflict.

5. **"The Teahouse Fire" by Ellis Avery**  
   - This novel explores the life of an American orphan in Japan who becomes part of a traditional tea ceremony family, offering insights into Japanese customs and society.

### Non-Fiction

1. **"Embracing Defeat: Japan in the Wake of World War II" by John W. Dower**  
   - A comprehensive analysis of the Japanese experience during the American occupation after World War II, examining the transformation of Japanese society and culture.

2. **"Hirohito and the Making of Modern Japan" by Herbert P. Bix**  
   - This biography of Emperor Hirohito explores his role in Japan’s militaristic expansion and post-war reconstruction.

3. **"A Modern History of Japan: From Tokugawa Times to the Present" by Andrew Gordon**  
   - A detailed and accessible overview of Japan’s history from the Tokugawa shogunate to the present, providing context for understanding modern Japan.

4. **"The Chrysanthemum and the Sword: Patterns of Japanese Culture" by Ruth Benedict**  
   - Written during World War II, this book examines Japanese culture and social structure through the lens of cultural anthropology.

5. **"Bending Adversity: Japan and the Art of Survival" by David Pilling**  
   - A contemporary analysis of Japan's economy, politics, and society, focusing on how the country has adapted to modern challenges.

### Japanese Perspective on World War II

1. **"Japan at War: An Oral History" by Haruko Taya Cook and Theodore F. Cook**  
   - A collection of firsthand accounts from Japanese soldiers, civilians, and survivors, providing a personal perspective on Japan’s experiences during World War II.

2. **"The Rape of Nanking: The Forgotten Holocaust of World War II" by Iris Chang**  
   - Although primarily focused on the Chinese perspective, this book provides insights into the actions of the Japanese military and the impact of the war.

3. **"Hiroshima" by John Hersey**  
   - A journalistic account of the aftermath of the atomic bombing of Hiroshima, featuring personal stories from survivors and their experiences.

4. **"Shōwa: A History of Japan" by Shigeru Mizuki**  
   - A graphic novel series that chronicles the history of Japan throughout the Shōwa era, offering a unique visual perspective on events leading up to and following World War II.

These books should give you a well-rounded understanding of Japan's rich history and culture, as well as diverse perspectives on significant events such as World War II.
