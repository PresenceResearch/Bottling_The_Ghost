The site was transformed into a temple complex after Yoshimitsu’s death in 1408, in accordance with his wishes.

- **Religious Transformation**: After Yoshimitsu's death, the villa was converted into a Zen Buddhist temple, affiliated with the Rinzai sect. The temple was named Rokuon-ji, after Yoshimitsu's Buddhist title. Kinkaku-ji became a symbol of the Kitayama culture that flourished under Yoshimitsu’s patronage, which blended elements of courtly elegance and the warrior class.
