The four essential principles associated with the Japanese tea ceremony, or *chanoyu* (茶の湯), are:

1. **和 (Wa)** - Harmony
2. **敬 (Kei)** - Respect
3. **清 (Sei)** - Purity
4. **寂 (Jaku)** - Tranquility

These principles guide the practice and philosophy of the tea ceremony, emphasizing the importance of harmony with others, respect for the tools and participants, purity of both mind and environment, and the tranquility that comes from the practice.
