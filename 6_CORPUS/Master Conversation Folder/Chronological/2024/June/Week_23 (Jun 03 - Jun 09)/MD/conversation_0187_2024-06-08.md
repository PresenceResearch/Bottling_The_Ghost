Salespeople in the commercial solar industry are typically judged by a variety of metrics for their bonus structure. These metrics are designed to align their performance with the company's strategic goals and drive business growth. Common metrics include:

1. **Sales Revenue**: The most straightforward metric, sales revenue, measures the total value of the deals closed by the salesperson. Higher revenue targets often result in higher bonuses.

2. **Number of New Clients**: This metric focuses on the ability of the salesperson to bring in new business. It can be measured by the number of new accounts opened or new contracts signed within a specific period.

3. **Project Completion**: Some compensation structures may include bonuses for successfully bringing projects to completion, ensuring that installations are done on time and within budget.

4. **Profit Margin**: Salespeople might be incentivized based on the profit margins of the deals they close. This encourages them to negotiate favorable terms and focus on higher-margin projects.

5. **Customer Satisfaction**: Customer satisfaction scores or Net Promoter Scores (NPS) can be used to gauge how well the salesperson maintains client relationships and delivers on customer expectations.

6. **Lead Conversion Rate**: This metric measures the effectiveness of the salesperson in converting leads into paying customers. A higher conversion rate typically results in higher bonuses.

7. **Quota Achievement**: Many sales roles include specific sales quotas. Bonuses are often tied to the percentage of quota achieved, with additional incentives for exceeding targets.

8. **Retention and Upselling**: Metrics that focus on retaining existing customers and upselling additional services or products can also be part of the bonus structure. This includes renewing contracts or increasing the size of existing projects.

9. **Pipeline Management**: Effective management of the sales pipeline, including the number of prospects in various stages of the sales process, can be a key performance indicator.

10. **Team Collaboration**: In some organizations, teamwork and collaboration with other departments (such as marketing or project management) can be a metric, encouraging a holistic approach to achieving business goals.

By using a combination of these metrics, companies can ensure that their salespeople are not only focused on closing deals but also on maintaining high levels of customer satisfaction, profitability, and long-term business growth.
