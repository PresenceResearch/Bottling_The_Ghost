#### **1. Pre-Call Preparation (Optional but Recommended)**
   - **Research Client's Business and Priorities**: Identify any relevant grants, incentives, or specific needs, especially for nonprofits or specialized industries.
   - **Prepare Financial and Suitability Visuals**: Have a model or heat map ready for on-the-spot site assessment. 
   - **Know Available Financing Options**: Be ready with details for ownership, PPAs, tax incentives, and any specific grant programs.

#### **2. Introduction (3-5 Minutes)**
   - **Set the Stage**: Briefly introduce yourself, your role, and Lumina Solar's capabilities.
   - **Agenda and Purpose**: Clearly outline the call’s purpose, explaining that you’ll be covering suitability, financial options, and any grant programs, and invite the client to ask questions anytime.

#### **3. Rapport Building (5-10 Minutes)**
   - **Establish Common Ground**: Engage personally by asking about their business’s background or a specific detail (e.g., location, previous energy solutions).
   - **Highlight Relevant Experience**: Briefly mention any similar projects you’ve handled that align with their industry or energy goals.
   - **Acknowledge Time Constraints**: Validate their busy schedule and emphasize that Lumina will handle most logistics, minimizing their involvement.

#### **4. Exploration and Discovery (15-20 Minutes)**
   - **Metrics (M)**: Uncover their cost-saving goals, electricity usage, and any operational efficiencies they hope to achieve.
   - **Economic Buyer (E)**: Identify all decision-makers, especially for nonprofits or commercial clients, and understand any specific expectations they have (e.g., ROI, grant dependency).
   - **Decision Criteria (D)**: Ask what criteria they’re using to evaluate solar providers and how Lumina can meet or exceed these benchmarks.
   - **Decision Process (D)**: Understand their approval process, expected timelines, and any stakeholders involved at each stage.
   - **Identify Pain Points (I)**: Probe into energy cost challenges, budget restrictions, and site limitations. Highlight the impact if these aren’t addressed, which may create urgency.

#### **5. Financial and Site Suitability Assessment (10-15 Minutes)**
   - **Site Suitability**: Review the roof, meter locations, and surrounding obstructions using visual aids (e.g., 3D models, heat maps).
   - **Investment Model**: Provide options for ownership, PPA, or leasing based on their financial profile. Discuss benefits, tax incentives, and grants for each model.
   - **Address Roof Concerns**: Clarify if roof upgrades are needed and how costs can be minimized through grants or financing options.

#### **6. Overcoming Objections (As Needed)**
   - **Clarify Complex Financing Options**: Address any confusion about PPAs, ownership, tax incentives, and grants. Summarize these clearly, comparing costs, benefits, and timelines.
   - **Explain Third-Party Ownership and Grant Funding**: For clients concerned about third-party ownership, explain benefits, maintenance, and long-term options (e.g., buyout or extension).
   - **Anticipate Common Objections**: Prepare responses to questions on roof integrity, maintenance, and grant disbursements to ensure transparency.

#### **7. Summarize and Confirm Key Insights (3-5 Minutes)**
   - **Recap Major Points**: Briefly summarize key takeaways from the conversation, including suitability, financial options, grant eligibility, and any specific client preferences or criteria.
   - **Validate Understanding**: Check in with the client to confirm that they understand the next steps and how the discussed options meet their needs.

#### **8. Next Steps and Timeline (5 Minutes)**
   - **Outline Immediate Actions**: For example, gathering energy bills, sending a site survey team, or preparing a grant application.
   - **Set a Follow-Up Date**: Propose a specific date for the next meeting to discuss final proposals or grant updates.

#### **9. Closing Remarks (2-3 Minutes)**
   - **Thank the Client**: Express appreciation for their time and interest, reiterating Lumina’s commitment to making the process as efficient and beneficial as possible.
   - **Reinforce Value and Dedication**: Close by emphasizing Lumina Solar’s dedication to optimizing their energy savings, handling logistics, and securing grants.

### **Additional Tips for Reps**
   - **Visualize Financial Benefits**: Use examples of savings from similar clients or highlight recent successful grant-funded projects to build credibility.
   - **Customize Discussion Points**: Adjust questions based on the client’s industry, location, and energy priorities to show you understand their specific needs.
   - **Be Proactive in Addressing Common Concerns**: Anticipate and respond to questions on roof suitability, financing options, and contract terms.
