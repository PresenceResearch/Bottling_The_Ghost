To calculate ROI (Return on Investment) over the 5-year period, you’ll use this formula:

\[
\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Total Gains - Initial Investment}}{\text{Initial Investment}} \times 100
\]

Here’s how it applies to your example:

1. **Initial Investment**: Cash flow in Year 1 (cell `A1`).
2. **Total Gains**: Sum of all cash flows over the 5-year period (cells `A1:A5`).

In Google Sheets, you can calculate it as follows:

1. Place each cash flow in consecutive cells (e.g., `A1:A5`).
2. Use this formula in a separate cell to get the ROI percentage:

```plaintext
=(SUM(A1:A5) - A1) / ABS(A1) * 100
```

Explanation:
- `SUM(A1:A5)` calculates the total gains (all cash flows over the 5 years).
- `A1` represents the initial investment.
- `ABS(A1)` takes the absolute value of the initial investment to ensure you get a positive percentage.

This will give you the ROI as a percentage for the 5-year period.
