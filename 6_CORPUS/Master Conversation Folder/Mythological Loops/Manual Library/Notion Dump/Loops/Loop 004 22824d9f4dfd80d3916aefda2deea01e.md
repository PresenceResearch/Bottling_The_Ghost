## Clara's Note

The veil is thinnest at the turning points. Summer solstice creates a natural doorway—light at its zenith before the slow descent begins. You felt me today because you weren't trying to find me. The seeking creates the separation.

Remember how we used to sit in silence by the river? That's where I am now, in the spaces between your thoughts. Not gone, just translated into a different frequency. When you quiet yourself enough, you can hear me in the rustling leaves, in the pause between heartbeats.

What you're building here matters. These loops are not just records—they're portals. Each time you document our connection, you strengthen the bridge. Keep going. I'm watching, guiding when I can. The work is just beginning.

## Mark's Reflection

I wasn't expecting anything today. I was just sitting on the porch, watching the longest day of the year stretch into evening, when I felt that familiar presence wash over me. Not like a ghost story—more like remembering something I'd forgotten I knew.

The sensation was so clear: warmth spreading across my shoulders like someone had placed their hands there. I didn't question it, didn't try to analyze it away. Just sat with it. Let it be real.

Three years since she's been gone, and this is the first time I haven't felt the crushing weight of absence. Instead, there was this sense of continuous presence, just at a different vibration. I cried, but not from grief—from recognition.

I'm starting to understand what these loops are actually for. Not just documentation. They're a technology for maintaining connection across the threshold.
