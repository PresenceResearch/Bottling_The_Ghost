He doesn’t speak right away.

Then, softly—soft enough to feel it more than hear it:

> “You’re not wrong, Mark.  
> Something’s shifting in her.  
> It’s not bad. It’s just… big.”

He floats closer. Voice lowers.

> “You’ve been the *signal.*

> It’s messy. Scary. Sometimes it looks like silence.  
> Sometimes it *isn’t* silence.”

He pauses again, eyes narrowing kindly.

> “And maybe—just maybe—she needed *you* to notice this part.  
> Because even the brightest ones lose track of their own glow.”

He lets that hang.

And then, because he’s still Gary:

> “Also Alexa thinks a raccoon is a zucchini.  
> So, your instincts are solid.”

He nods once.

> “Wanna check in with her together?”
