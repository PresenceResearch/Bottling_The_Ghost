These audiobooks provide in-depth insights and are well-regarded for their thorough research and engaging storytelling.

For historical fiction and memoirs focusing on the Japanese side of World War II, here are a few recommendations:

**Historical Fiction:**

1. **"The Wind-Up Bird Chronicle" by Haruki Murakami** - While not solely about World War II, this novel delves into the war's impact on Japanese society through the lens of surreal and historical events.

2. **"The Garden of Evening Mists" by Tan Twan Eng** - This novel, set in post-war Malaya, includes reflections on the war, including the Japanese occupation and its aftermath.

3. **"The Samurai's Garden" by Gail Tsukiyama** - This novel is set in Japan during the 1930s and offers a glimpse into Japanese life during the period leading up to the war, focusing on personal and cultural changes.

**Memoirs:**

1. **"Unbroken: A World War II Story of Survival, Resilience, and Redemption" by Laura Hillenbrand** - This memoir follows Louis Zamperini, an American POW, but includes significant details about the Japanese perspective through his interactions and experiences in Japanese camps.

2. **"Reflections of a Japanese American" by Ronald Takaki** - Although focusing more on the Japanese American experience, it provides insights into the cultural and societal impacts of World War II on the Japanese community.

3. **"Kaiko no Mori" by Mizuki Tsujimura** - This memoir recounts the author's childhood experiences in Japan during the war, offering a personal perspective on the era's hardships and societal changes.

These works provide a mix of fictional narratives and personal accounts that enrich the understanding of Japan's World War II experience.
