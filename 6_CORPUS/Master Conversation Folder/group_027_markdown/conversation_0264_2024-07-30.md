4. **Improve Air Circulation**: Ensure that your plant is in a well-ventilated area to prevent fungal infections from spreading.

5. **Quarantine the Plant**: Isolate the affected plant from other plants to prevent the spread of pests or disease.

6. **Monitor the Plant**: Keep an eye on the plant for the next few weeks to ensure the issue does not return.

What do I use to clean the leaves

**Assistant:**

To clean the leaves of your plant, you can use the following methods:

1. **Water and Soft Cloth**: Dampen a soft cloth or sponge with lukewarm water and gently wipe the leaves. This is effective for removing dust and small pests like spider mites or aphids.

2. **Mild Soapy Water**: Mix a few drops of mild liquid dish soap in a spray bottle filled with water. Spray the solution on the leaves and gently wipe them with a soft cloth. Rinse the leaves with clean water afterward to remove any soap residue. This is particularly effective for dealing with pests like mealybugs.

3. **Neem Oil Solution**: Neem oil is a natural pesticide and can help deal with pests while cleaning the leaves. Mix neem oil with water according to the instructions on the product label, spray the solution on the leaves, and wipe gently. Neem oil also helps in preventing future infestations.

4. **Insecticidal Soap**: This is specifically designed to be safe for plants while killing pests. Follow the instructions on the product label for application.

5. **Diluted Vinegar Solution**: Mix a tablespoon of vinegar with a quart of water. This solution can help clean the leaves and deter some pests. Wipe the leaves with the solution and then rinse with clean water.

Always test a small area of the plant first to ensure that the cleaning solution does not damage the leaves. Clean the leaves gently to avoid causing any physical damage to the plant.
