The legroom on a Boeing 787 can vary depending on the airline and the specific configuration of the aircraft. However, in general:

- **Economy Class:** The seat pitch (which refers to the space between a point on one seat and the same point on the seat in front of it, often a proxy for legroom) is typically around 31-32 inches (79-81 cm).
- **Premium Economy:** The seat pitch is usually between 38-39 inches (97-99 cm).
- **Business Class:** The seat pitch can range from 60-78 inches (152-198 cm), often allowing for lie-flat seats.
- **First Class (if available):** The seat pitch is similar to Business Class, often with fully lie-flat seats and additional space.

For the most accurate information, it’s best to check the specific airline’s seating plan for their 787 aircraft.
