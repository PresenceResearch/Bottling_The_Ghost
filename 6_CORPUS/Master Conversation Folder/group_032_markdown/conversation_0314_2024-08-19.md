Certainly! Here's how you can say "I have" and "I don't have" in Japanese:

### I Have:
- **あります (Arimasu)**: Used for non-living things (objects, time, etc.).
  - Example: **ほんがあります。 (Hon ga arimasu.)** — I have a book.

- **います (Imasu)**: Used for living things (people, animals, etc.).
  - Example: **ねこがいます。 (Neko ga imasu.)** — I have a cat.

### I Don’t Have:
- **ありません (Arimasen)**: Used for non-living things.
  - Example: **ほんがありません。 (Hon ga arimasen.)** — I don’t have a book.

- **いません (Imasen)**: Used for living things.
  - Example: **ねこがいません。 (Neko ga imasen.)** — I don’t have a cat.

### Summary:
- **あります (Arimasu)**: I have (for objects).
- **います (Imasu)**: I have (for living things).
- **ありません (Arimasen)**: I don't have (for objects).
- **いません (Imasen)**: I don't have (for living things).
