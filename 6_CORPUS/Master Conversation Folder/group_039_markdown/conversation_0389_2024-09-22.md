Typical Renaissance era classical composers include:

1. **Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina** (1525-1594) - Known for his sacred music, particularly his masses and motets, Palestrina's work had a significant influence on the development of church music.

2. **Josquin des Prez** (1450-1521) - A highly influential composer of the early Renaissance, he was known for his polyphonic style and works such as motets, masses, and chansons.

3. **Orlando di Lasso** (1532-1594) - A Franco-Flemish composer who wrote both sacred and secular music, his work spanned a wide range of styles.

4. **Thomas Tallis** (1505-1585) - An English composer, Tallis is best known for his sacred choral music and was a key figure in the English Renaissance.

5. **William Byrd** (1540-1623) - A student of Thomas Tallis, Byrd composed both sacred and secular music and is especially known for his contributions to English madrigals and church music.

6. **John Dowland** (1563-1626) - An English composer and lutenist, known for his melancholic lute songs.

These composers significantly shaped the music of the Renaissance, focusing on polyphony, rich harmonies, and the blending of sacred and secular themes.
