An acceptable ROI for a solar project typically depends on the project type, location, financing structure, and goals, but general guidelines can help gauge what’s considered a solid return. Here’s a breakdown:

### 1. **Residential Solar**
   - **Payback Period**: Commonly around 7-10 years, with systems lasting 25-30 years, meaning 15-20 years of ‘free’ electricity.
   - **ROI**: 8-15% annually is considered good for residential projects. Some projects may hit higher ROIs if they benefit from state incentives, favorable net metering, or high electricity costs.

### 2. **Commercial Solar**
   - **Payback Period**: Often ranges from 5-8 years, sometimes less if incentives like tax credits, grants, or accelerated depreciation are applied.
   - **ROI**: Aiming for 10-20% annually is common. With financing options, ROI can jump if costs are front-loaded and financed, while incentives and savings build over time.

### 3. **Utility-Scale Solar**
   - **Payback Period**: Varies greatly but generally falls between 6-12 years, depending on the size and the power purchase agreement (PPA) terms.
   - **ROI**: Utility projects often target an annualized ROI of around 6-12%. Margins can be tight, so securing long-term PPAs or participating in lucrative renewable energy credit (REC) markets becomes crucial.

### Key Factors Affecting Solar ROI:
   - **Incentives**: Federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC), local tax breaks, grants, and SRECs can boost ROI considerably.
   - **Electricity Rates**: Higher rates and rapid utility escalation can drive better ROI since savings directly correlate with energy costs.
   - **Location**: Sunlight exposure, grid policies, and state incentives can significantly affect the financial viability of solar projects.
   - **Financing Structure**: Cash purchases typically have a slower payback but higher overall ROI, whereas financed projects often show a quicker ROI despite higher upfront costs.

### Acceptable Thresholds:
   - **Minimum ROI**: For most commercial and residential solar projects, a minimum annual ROI of 7-10% is often targeted, though projects with 10-15% are typically considered very strong investments.
   - **Benchmark Payback**: Payback periods under 10 years are commonly viewed as acceptable, though quicker paybacks are always a plus for investors.

These ROI targets make solar projects generally appealing, especially with stable returns and government-backed incentives, but expectations can vary by market and investor priorities.
