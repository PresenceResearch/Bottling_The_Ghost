A **regression model** is a type of statistical or machine learning model used to predict a continuous outcome variable (dependent variable) based on one or more input variables (independent variables). It is widely used in various fields such as finance, healthcare, and social sciences to understand relationships between variables and make predictions.

### Key Concepts of Regression Models

1. **Continuous Output**: 
   - Unlike classification models that predict discrete categories, regression models predict continuous numerical values, such as house prices, temperatures, or sales figures.

2. **Input Variables**:
   - These are the independent variables (also known as features or predictors) that the model uses to make predictions.

3. **Types of Regression Models**:
   - **Linear Regression**: Assumes a linear relationship between the independent variables and the dependent variable.
     \[
     y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x_1 + \beta_2 x_2 + \dots + \beta_n x_n + \epsilon
     \]
     where \(y\) is the predicted outcome, \(x_1, x_2, \dots\) are the input variables, \(\beta\) are the coefficients, and \(\epsilon\) is the error term.
   - **Polynomial Regression**: Models non-linear relationships by including polynomial terms (e.g., \(x^2, x^3\)).
   - **Logistic Regression**: Despite its name, it is used for binary classification.
   - **Ridge and Lasso Regression**: Include regularization to prevent overfitting by penalizing large coefficients.
   - **Non-Linear Regression**: Models more complex relationships that cannot be captured by a straight line.
   - **Support Vector Regression (SVR)**: Extends support vector machines for regression tasks.
   - **Neural Network Regression**: Uses deep learning to capture complex patterns.

4. **Key Metrics to Evaluate Regression Models**:
   - **Mean Absolute Error (MAE)**: Average of absolute errors.
   - **Mean Squared Error (MSE)**: Average of squared errors.
   - **Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)**: Square root of MSE, to measure error in the same unit as the dependent variable.
   - **R-squared (\(R^2\))**: Proportion of variance in the dependent variable explained by the independent variables.

5. **Applications**:
   - Predicting housing prices based on size, location, and other factors.
   - Forecasting sales or revenue based on historical data.
   - Estimating a patient's health outcome based on medical history and test results.
