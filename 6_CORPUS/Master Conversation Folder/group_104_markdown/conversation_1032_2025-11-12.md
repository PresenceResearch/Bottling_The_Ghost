## **The Three Ways People Use AI for Photos (Only One Is Worth It)**

### **1. Let AI auto-tag and sort everything for you**  
This is what Google Photos and Apple Photos already do. It’s good, but you can’t *direct* it — it’s passive.  
Useful for:  
- Find all pictures of Caroline instantly  
- Find “me and Shelagh at a concert”  
- Locate years of cat photos you forgot you took

But it doesn’t fix *organization*, just retrieval.

### **2. Use AI to build albums, storylines, and “best of” folders**  
This is slightly better — you dump everything in, and the AI creates curated sets.  
Examples:  
- “Show me the 100 best photos from the Italy trip”  
- “Build an album of every photo where my daughter looks genuinely happy”  
- “Pull all selfies and rank by quality”

This works if you’re in Google Photos or Apple Photos. Not customizable beyond prompts.

### **3. The *actual move*: A local AI model that indexes your entire photo library with natural-language search**  
This is the one people don’t know about yet.  
This means:  
- Your photos stay on your laptop/drive  
- A local AI (like PhotoPrism or Immich + an open-source vision model) tags, clusters, and embeds everything  
- You query your own library however you want  
- “Find the picture where I’m wearing the blue flannel holding a coffee cup at the lake”  
- “Show me every photo with my old beagle from 2015–2017”  
- “Pull every image with a whiteboard”  
- “Find that one picture where Shelagh was laughing at the bar — mid-motion”

This is the point where it actually becomes *useful* instead of novelty.

It turns your whole photo archive into a searchable memory.
